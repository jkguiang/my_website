About Me

I was born on June 19th, 1997 in San Diego, California and am currently studying Physics at the University of California Santa Barbara. While working for the Campagnari High Energy Physics group at UCSB, I have gained a greater interest in and appreciation for coding. I am most comfortable in Python, but I have a working knowledge of C++ and am familiar with several other programming languages including JavaScript, html, and bash. My work for the Campagnari group is mainly computational. We use ROOT, a C++ framework designed by and for physicists to parse and interpret data gathered by the CMS detector at the LHC. Outside of my scientific interests, I am an amateur graphic artist and play the piano and guitar (poorly). I also love doing anything active; whether it's running, lifting weights, or hiking, indoors or outdoors, I love to exercise. As far as my other persuits, I have done some more serious graphic design for some small companies and startups. Examples of all of my work can be found on this website.
